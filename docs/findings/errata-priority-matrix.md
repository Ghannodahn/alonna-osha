# Errata Priority Matrix

## Purpose

This matrix ranks identified safety content gaps to determine which errata to develop first. Not everything requires immediate attention - this prioritization ensures effort goes toward issues that matter most.

---

## Scoring Methodology

Each gap is evaluated on four dimensions (1-5 scale):

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Injury Severity** | Minor (scrapes, bruises) | Moderate (sprains, cuts requiring care) | Severe (fractures, fatalities, permanent injury) |
| **Incident Frequency** | Rare (< 1% of volunteers) | Occasional (1-5% of volunteers) | Common (> 5% of volunteers or daily exposure) |
| **Volunteer Confusion** | Rarely misunderstood | Sometimes confusing | Frequent questions or misapplication |
| **Ease of Addressing** | Requires major restructuring or external resources | Moderate effort (new section, research needed) | Quick fix (clarification, rewording, simple addition) |

**Composite Score** = (Severity x 2) + Frequency + Confusion + (6 - Ease)

*Note: Ease is inverted - easier fixes score higher because they offer better ROI.*

## P0: Critical Priority (Do First)

These gaps represent high-severity hazards with poor or missing coverage. Address before the next volunteer event.

| Gap | Severity | Frequency | Confusion | Ease | **Total** | Content Status |
|-----|----------|-----------|-----------|------|-----------|----------------|
| **1. Electrical Safety** | 5 | 3 | 4 | 3 | **20** | Missing entirely |
| **2. Fall Protection (beyond ladders)** | 5 | 4 | 3 | 3 | **20** | Missing entirely |
| **3. Emergency Procedures** | 5 | 2 | 5 | 4 | **19** | Vague reference only |
| **4. Struck-By/Working Above-Below** | 5 | 3 | 3 | 4 | **18** | Discovery doc ready |
| **5. Heat Illness Prevention** | 5 | 3 | 3 | 3 | **18** | Missing entirely |

### Rationale

1. **Electrical Safety (Score: 20)**: Zero coverage of a Focus Four hazard. Electrocution causes 8% of construction fatalities. Volunteers encounter extension cords, power tools, and temporary power daily. One sentence about "check cords for damage" is not safety guidance.

2. **Fall Protection Beyond Ladders (Score: 20)**: Falls are #1 cause of construction deaths (39%). Handbook covers ladders but ignores 6-foot rule, scaffold basics, edge hazards, and guardrails. Volunteers may encounter elevated surfaces without understanding risks.

3. **Emergency Procedures (Score: 19)**: Current guidance: "notify the instructor and site coordinator immediately." No exit locations, assembly points, emergency numbers, or chain of command. During an actual emergency, this guidance is useless.

4. **Struck-By/Working Above-Below (Score: 18)**: ~10% of construction fatalities (2023 BLS). No mention of hard hat zones, drop zones, tool tethering, or communication protocols. Discovery document (09-working-above-below.md) has content ready for integration.

5. **Heat Illness Prevention (Score: 18)**: Zero coverage. 50-70% of outdoor heat fatalities occur in first few days of exposure - exactly the profile of volunteers. Becoming an OSHA enforcement priority. Seasonal but critical.

## P1: High Priority (30-Day Target)

Important gaps that affect safety but aren't immediately life-threatening, or have content partially ready.

| Gap | Severity | Frequency | Confusion | Ease | **Total** | Content Status |
|-----|----------|-----------|-----------|------|-----------|----------------|
| **6. Manual Handling/Lifting** | 4 | 5 | 4 | 4 | **17** | Discovery doc ready |
| **7. PPE - When/Why/How** | 3 | 5 | 5 | 4 | **16** | Needs restructuring |
| **8. Job Hazard Analysis Thinking** | 3 | 5 | 4 | 3 | **16** | Discovery doc ready |

| **11. Incident Reporting Guidance** | 2 | 3 | 4 | 4 | **14** | Missing entirely |
| **12. First Aid Specifics** | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | **14** | Vague reference only |

### Rationale

6. **Manual Handling (Score: 17)**: "Lift with legs, not back" is insufficient. MSDs are a leading cause of construction injuries, with nearly half of workers reporting symptoms. Discovery document 07-manual-handling-golden-rule.md has comprehensive content ready.

7. **PPE Section (Score: 16)**: Lists 9 items without context. Creates confusion (do I wear all 9 always?) and compliance fatigue. Needs restructuring into baseline/task-specific/optional.

8. **JHA Thinking (Score: 16)**: Most injuries happen when workers don't anticipate hazards. No pre-task assessment guidance exists. Discovery document 08-job-hazard-analysis.md has content ready.

9. **Ladder Safety Expansion (Score: 16)**: Good foundation exists but missing: 4-to-1 ratio, 3-foot extension rule, weight ratings, setup on uneven ground. Content framework is ready; needs completion.

10. **Role Clarity (Score: 15)**: "Notify the instructor" appears multiple times. Who is that person? Where are they? Easy fix with high confusion reduction.

11. **Incident Reporting (Score: 14)**: No guidance on what to report, when, or why it matters. Affects organizational learning.

12. **First Aid Specifics (Score: 14)**: "First aid kits will be located on site or in the storage trailer" - where exactly? What's inside? When to escalate?

## Appendix: Scoring Details

### Injury Severity Calibration
- **5**: Can cause death or permanent disability (electrocution, severe falls, crushing)
- **4**: Can cause serious injury requiring medical care (fractures, severe cuts, heat stroke)
- **3**: Can cause moderate injury (sprains, cuts, heat exhaustion)
- **2**: Can cause minor injury (bruises, minor cuts, muscle strain)
- **1**: Unlikely to cause physical injury (confusion, inefficiency)

### Incident Frequency Calibration
- **5**: Volunteers encounter this hazard every work session
- **4**: Volunteers encounter this hazard most work sessions
- **3**: Volunteers encounter this hazard occasionally
- **2**: Volunteers rarely encounter this specific hazard
- **1**: Hazard is uncommon in typical volunteer work

### Volunteer Confusion Calibration
- **5**: Volunteers regularly misunderstand or misapply guidance
- **4**: Volunteers often have questions about this topic
- **3**: Volunteers sometimes need clarification
- **2**: Volunteers generally understand with minimal explanation
- **1**: Volunteers rarely have questions

### Ease of Addressing Calibration
- **5**: Quick clarification or rewording; content exists
- **4**: Moderate addition; discovery content available
- **3**: New section needed; research required
- **2**: Significant new content; possible external expertise
- **1**: Major restructuring; graphics/video production needed
