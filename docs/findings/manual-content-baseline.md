## Section 1: Introduction & Program Overview

**Pages:** 1-2
**Coverage Rating:** ◐ Informational (not safety content)

### Content Summary

| Component | Description | Word Count |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Welcome Statement | Safety as priority, "common sense" mention | ~50 |
| Learning Objectives | 6 items covering tools, safety, hazards | ~40 |
| Class Activities | 7 items including safety discussion | ~50 |
| Tool Lists | 3 categories (participant, home, volunteer) | ~80 |

### Safety-Relevant Content

- **Philosophy statement**: "Safety and health of workers and volunteers is the number one priority"
- **General principle**: "A little common sense goes a long way"
- **Tone**: Encouraging but vague on specifics

### Assessment

This section establishes safety culture intent but provides no actionable safety guidance. The "common sense" framing is problematic for inexperienced volunteers who lack construction context.

### Safety Philosophy Statement

> - Safety prioritized over productivity
> - Report all hazards and injuries

### Assessment

PPE items are listed and pictured but selection guidance is minimal. Only safety glasses, gloves, dust mask, and footwear have explicit "when to use" statements. Critical gaps:
- Hard hat requirements never specified
- Hearing protection timing never specified
- PPE inspection not covered
- PPE fit not covered

## Section 8: Ladder Safety

**Page:** 12
**Coverage Rating:** ◕ Good (70%)

### Complete Rule List

| # | Rule | OSHA Alignment |
|---|------|----------------|
| 1 | Second person required to hold ladder (Building Beyond the Walls policy) | Exceeds OSHA |
| 2 | Inspect ladder before use | ✓ 29 CFR 1926.1053 |
| 3 | Ensure ladder is sturdy before climbing | ✓ |
| 4 | Maintain 3-point contact (2 hands + 1 foot OR 2 feet + 1 hand) | ✓ Well-explained |
| 5 | Keep body within side rails; never overreach | ✓ |
| 6 | Don't use top step/rung unless designed for it | ✓ |
| 7 | Never put one foot on rung and one on another surface | ✓ Specific |
| 8 | Use edge stabilizers on sharp edges (gutters) | ✓ |

### Assessment

**Best-covered topic in the manual.** The 3-point contact explanation is particularly clear. The second-person requirement exceeds OSHA minimums and is a smart organizational control.

### Remaining Gaps

| Missing Element | OSHA Requirement |
|-----------------|------------------|
| 4-to-1 angle rule | Standard practice |
| 3-foot extension above landing | 29 CFR 1926.1053(b)(1) |
| Weight rating selection | Capacity matching |
| Setup on uneven ground | Common site issue |
| Material handling at height | Use rope/bucket |

## Section 10: Summary & Disclaimer

**Page:** 13
**Coverage Rating:** N/A (administrative)

### Key Statements

| Statement | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| "IN NO WAY THIS BRIEF SUMMARY CAN COVER ALL THE SAFETY ISSUES ON A JOB SITE" | Disclaimer |
| Instructor will provide site-specific tips | Deferral |
| Review notes before reporting to site | Reinforcement |
| "REMEMBER-ALWAYS EXERCISE CAUTION AND COMMON SENSE!" | Encouragement |
| "PLEASE KEEP SAFETY IN MIND AND WE WILL ALL HAVE FUN AND ENJOY THE CLASS TOGETHER" | Encouragement |

### Assessment

The disclaimer undermines confidence in the training ("this can't cover everything"). Better approach: direct volunteers to ask questions rather than signal inadequacy.

## Methodology Notes

This baseline was compiled from:
1. Direct transcription of manual photographs (13 HEIC images converted to WebP)
2. Comparison against OSHA 29 CFR 1926 construction standards
3. Cross-reference with Focus Four hazard categories
4. Assessment using [Covered Topics Analysis](covered-topics-analysis.md) depth ratings

**Legend for Coverage Ratings:**
- ● Complete (90%+)
- ◕ Good (60-89%)
- ◐ Partial (30-59%)
- ◔ Minimal (<30%)
