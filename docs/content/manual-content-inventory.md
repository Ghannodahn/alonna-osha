### Pages 10-12: ACTUAL SAFETY RULES (Errata Target)

**Page 10 - Personal Safety (12 rules)**
1. First aid kit location awareness
2. Breathing hazards require mask
3. Treated lumber requires mask and gloves; baby powder helps
4. Wash hands after handling materials
5. Heavy materials: always use legs, not back
6. Proper gloves for welding
7. Safety glasses with power tools, sanding, nailing, hammering
8. Shields always down on job site
9. Clothing: no loose fitting, long sleeves, no dangling jewelry
10. Nail-carrying bags: leather, canvas, or plastic; no shorts
11. Awareness of surroundings
12. Awareness of surroundings at all times (repeated emphasis)

**Page 10 - Hand Tool Safety (3 rules)**
1. Watch for person next to you
2. Distance from yourself; aware of fingers vs blade
3. Damaged tools: put aside, bring to attention

**Page 10-11 - Power Tool Safety (7+ rules)**
1. Power tools NOT allowed without Building Beyond the Walls training
2. Instruction required before use
3. Check cords for damage; don't pull cord to unplug
4. Proper cord storage (figure 8, not wrapped on tool)
5. Blade guards must work properly
6. Check all power cords before bringing out
7. Extension cord protection on ground

**Page 12 - Ladder Safety (8 rules)**
1. Building Beyond the Walls requires ladder safety accord
2. Inspect before use
3. Always steady before climbing
4. 3-point contact (two hands/one foot OR two feet/one hand)
5. Keep body within side rails; never overreach
6. Don't use top 2 steps or place on uneven surface
7. Never put one foot on rung and one on another surface
8. Use edge stabilizers on sharp edges (e.g., gutters)

**Page 12 - Maintenance Safety (4 rules)**
1. Clean work site: no tripping hazards
2. Keep track of tools; return misplaced items
3. Trash properly
4. Sharp edge tools stored safely

**Page 13 - Summary/Disclaimer**
- "IN NO WAY THIS BRIEF SUMMARY CAN COVER ALL THE SAFETY NEEDS ON A JOB SITE"
- Instructor must provide site-specific information
- Must review notes from this manual before reporting to site
- "REMEMBER-ALWAYS EXERCISE CAUTION AND COMMON SENSE!"
- "PLEASE KEEP SAFETY IN MIND AND WE WILL ALL HAVE FUN AND ENJOY THE CLASS TOGETHER"

## Content Gap Analysis

### What the Manual Covers Well
1. Tool identification and naming
2. Basic PPE listing
3. Restricted tool policies (nail guns, powder actuated)
4. General safety mindset

### What the Manual Lacks (Errata Opportunities)

| Gap Category | Missing Content |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Job Hazard Analysis** | No mention of JHA, hazard assessment before work |
| **Working at Heights** | Beyond ladders - scaffolding, fall protection |
| **Working Below Others** | Overhead hazard awareness, exclusion zones |
| **Electrical Safety** | GFCI requirements, wet conditions, lockout/tagout |
| **Heat/Cold Stress** | Hydration, heat exhaustion recognition |
| **Lifting Specifics** | Team lifts, weight limits, awkward loads |
| **Tool-Specific Safety** | Detailed procedures for each power tool |
| **Emergency Response** | Beyond first aid kit location |
| **Chemical Safety** | Beyond treated lumber - adhesives, solvents |
| **Hearing Protection** | When required, decibel guidance |
| **Eye Protection** | Beyond "power tools" - specific activities |
| **Respiratory Protection** | Mask selection, fit, when required |
| **Struck-By Hazards** | Dropped tools, swinging materials |
| **Caught-In/Between** | Pinch points, crushing hazards |
| **Trench/Excavation** | If applicable to volunteer work |
